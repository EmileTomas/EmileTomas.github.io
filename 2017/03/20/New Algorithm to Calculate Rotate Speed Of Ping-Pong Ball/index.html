New Algorithm to Calculate Rotate Speed Of Ping-Pong Ball

Posted on 2017-03-20   |   In Research   |     |   visitors

Introduction

Nowadays, using computer vision technologies to help athlete measure their sports index is popular and useful. The data measured by these technologies is also used as data source for the vision system of sports robots and the training of neural networks.

The measurement of the rotate speed of Ping-Pong ball is difficult at present research, considering the rotational motion-blur and feature detect, whereas the measurement of 3-D movement of Ping-Pong ball is easy.

A database of the relation between rotational motion-blur and the 3-D movement is used to deblur in some research to solve the problem of motion-blur. Some special Ping-Pong balls are made to help the feature detection of the rotation, which can get a good result but is not practical.

In this article, we will introduce a new algorithm to calculate the rotation information of the Ping-Pong ball using its logo based of Hough Circle detection and SURF features of the ball. As what we will see, our representation can reach a high accuracy and fast speed.

Overview

To measure the rotation information of a ping-pong ball in a video, three steps need to be done. Find the ball in every frame, matching rotation feature between two frame, and calculate the rotation information at last.

In the following content, we will introduce the detail of each process, and also the test of the algorithm. At the last part, we will give some further thinking and reflection of the algorithm, and we will also conclude some future work need to be done.

Find the ball

One obvious way to find the ball is using circle detection, maybe color detection or motion detection can also help. In our algorithm, we use Hough-Circle detect to find the edge of the ball. The time complexity of this algorithm is $$ O(n^3)$$ in the number of the non-zero points of the image, so we crop a ROI from subsequent frames once we detect a circle in the present processing frame to make the program faster. ROI is decided by the circle we detected which is larger than the circle because of the movement of the ball in reality.

Feature Matching

After find the ball in different frame, we need match the features of two frames to get some information for next stage calculation. And we use the SURF features to fulfill the feature extraction, which has the feature of scale invariance and rotational invariance. The next step is to do feature matching after getting the feature of the ball.

Calculation

After the feature matching, we can get two set of matched points, each one from one frame. They may looks like:
$$
\left[
\begin{matrix}
x_1 & x_2 &x_3 \\
y_1 &y_2 & y_3 \\
z_1 & z_2 &z_3
\end{matrix}
\right]
$$
Each column of the matrix represent one point and then we can set to calculate the rotational information, the axis vector $\vec I=(\vec {x_0}, \vec {y_0},\vec {z_0})$ (supposing $(x_0,y_0,z_0)$is on the ball), and the rotation angle $\theta$.

At first, we note the points set from the first frame as $S_1$ and the set from second frame as $S_2$

$$
S_1=\left[
\begin{matrix}
x_1 & x_2 &x_3\\
y_1 &y_2 & y_3\\
z_1 & z_2 &z_3
\end{matrix}
\right]
\tag{1}
$$

$$
\\
S_2=\left[
\begin{matrix}
x_1^\prime & x_2^\prime &x_3^\prime\\
y_1^\prime &y_2^\prime & y_3^\prime\\
z_1^\prime & z_2 ^\prime&z_3^\prime
\end{matrix}
\right]
\tag{2}
$$

To rotate $S_1$ to $S_2$, we have the formulation below:
$$
T^{-1} \cdot R_x^{-1}(\alpha) \cdot R_y^{-1}(\beta) \cdot R_z(\theta) \cdot R_y(\beta) \cdot R_z(\alpha) \cdot T \cdot S_1= S_2 \tag{3}
$$
And because the coordinates of the points is supposing that the coordinate of ball center is (0,0,0), there is no need for translation matrix $T$.

And we take the transform matrix as $R(\theta)$ for convenience:

$$
R(\theta)= R_x^{-1}(\alpha) \cdot R_y^{-1}(\beta) \cdot R_z(\theta) \cdot R_y(\beta) \cdot R_z(\alpha) \tag{4}
$$

Then we get:
$$
R(\theta)=S_2 \cdot S_1^{-1} \tag{5}
$$

The right side of formulation (5) is computable from the feature matching sets, while the left side is composed by unknown number $x_0,y_0,z_0,\theta$. Because $(x_0,y_0,z_0)$ is on the ball surface, so we have:

$$
x_0^2+y_0^2+z_0^2=r^2
\tag{6}
$$

To represent $R_x(\alpha), R_y(\beta), R_z(\theta)$ more concise, we have short notes:
$$
a=\frac{x_0}{r} , b=\frac{y_0}{r},c=\frac{z_0}{r} \tag{7}
$$

From CG knowledge, we have:

$$
R_x(\alpha)=
\left[
\begin{matrix}
1 & 0 & 0 \\
0 & \frac{c}{d} & -\frac{b}{d} \\
0 & \frac{b}{d} & \frac{c}{d}
\end{matrix}
\right] \tag{8}
$$

$$
R_y(\beta)=
\left[
\begin{matrix}
d & 0 &-a \\
0 & 1 & 0 \\
a & 0 &d\\
\end{matrix}
\right] \tag{9}
$$

$$
R_z(\theta)=
\left[
\begin{matrix}
cos\theta & -sin\theta & 0\\
sin\theta & cos\theta & 0\\
0 & 0 &1
\end{matrix}
\right] \tag{10}
$$

After simplification, from (4) and (6) - (10), we have:
$$
R(\theta)=\frac{1}{r^2}
\left[
\begin{matrix}
x^2+cos\theta(r^2-x^2) &
xy(1-cos\theta)-rzsin\theta &
xz(1-cos\theta)+rysin\theta&\\
xy(1-cos\theta)+rzsin\theta &
y^2+cos\theta(r^2-y^2) &
yz(1-cos\theta)-rxsin\theta \\
xz(1-cos\theta)-rysin\theta &
yz(1-cos\theta)+rxsin\theta&
z^2+cos\theta(r^2-z^2)
\end{matrix}
\right] \tag{11}
$$

We already have calculated the right side of formulation (5), and also we get the simple expression of the left side. The only thing we need to do, is to solve a equation of size 9:
$$
k_x=R(\theta)(3,2)-R(\theta)(2,3)=\frac{2xsin\theta}{r} \tag{12}
$$
$$
k_y=R(\theta)(1,3)-R(\theta)(3,1)=\frac{2ysin\theta}{r} \tag{13}
$$
$$
k_z=R(\theta)(2,1)-R(\theta)(1,2)=\frac{2zsin\theta}{r} \tag{14}
$$
Result:
$$
\theta=arcsin\frac{r\sqrt{k_x^2+k_y^2+k_z^2}}{2}\\
x_0=r \cdot \frac{k_x}{\sqrt{k_x^2+k_y^2+k_z^2}}\\
y_0=r \cdot \frac{k_y}{\sqrt{k_x^2+k_y^2+k_z^2}}\\
z_0=r \cdot \frac{k_z}{\sqrt{k_x^2+k_y^2+k_z^2}}\\
$$
